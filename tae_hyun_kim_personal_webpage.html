Tae Hyun Kim 金泰炫

PhD in Chinese and Critical Theory, University of California, Berkeley

Profile

Dr. Tae Hyun Kim is an independent scholar specializing in premodern East Asian religions, intellectual history, and literature, with a particular focus on China, Korea, and their dynamic interactions.

As an author, he wrote Korean Neo Confucian Perspectives on Laozi and Zhuangzi (Three Pines Press, 2026) and is currently preparing another monograph on the religious landscape of premodern Korea, also expected to appear in 2026.

As a translator, he is currently translating several important premodern Korean and Chinese religious and literary classics into English, many of which remain largely unknown in Western scholarship.

As a scholar of Early China, he has conducted research on excavated manuscripts and archaeological discoveries at various universities in China and has produced numerous articles, book chapters, and conference presentations on early Chinese intellectual history, the creation of historical writing, the textual formation of received classics, and issues of historical memory in Early China.

As a professor, he has taught a wide range of courses on premodern China, premodern Korea, and East Asian Studies at several American universities, including the University of California, Berkeley; Washington University in St. Louis; and Emory University.
